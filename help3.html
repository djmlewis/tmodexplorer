Disclaimer & Acknowledgements

This is beta software and may experience bugs and crashes. Please (politely) report any bugs to d.j.lewis@surrey.ac.uk.

The software depends on the R package tmod 'Feature Set Enrichment Analysis for Metabolomics and Transcriptomics' (description), created by January Weiner. I have reverse engineered some aspects of tmod and any errors arising from this are mine. Consult the tmod documentation or vignette for a description of the modules.

The transcriptomics and cytokines datasets are from the BIOVACSAFE project (www.biovacsafe.eu), and were generated by the group of Stefan Kaufmann at MPIIB, Berlin and provided by Jeroen Maertzdorf and January Weiner. I have generated  averages per treatment ~ time point and fold-increases, and any errors there are mine. The cells data were generated by the Data Management team at Surrey and are GCP compliant, although the various means and fold-changes have been calculated by me.

The trials were conducted at the University of Gent by Geert Leroux-Roels, and at the University of Surrey by me.

Source files for the shiny app are available at www.github.com/djmlewis/tmodexplorer. The shiny app is online at djmlewis.shinyapps.io/tmodexplorer

tmodExplorer is written in R: R Core Team (2017). R: A language and environment for statistical computing. R Foundation for Statistical Computing, Vienna,
  Austria. URL https://www.R-project.org/.
tmodExplorer uses tidyverse packages (https://www.tidyverse.org) extensively especially dplyr, purrr, readr, ggplot2 and tibble.
Other packages to acknowledge are shineythemes, forcats, shinydashboard, shinyWidgets, shinyBS, shinyjs, grid, gridExtra, utf8, stringr, VennDiagnram, eulerr, UpSetR, qgraph, openxlsx, xtable and cowplot.
